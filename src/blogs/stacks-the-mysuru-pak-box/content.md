# Stacks: The Mysuru Pak Box 🍱

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Domain:** Data Structures & Algorithms

> "Bro, it's just like eating the top piece first!"

### The Majestic "Majestic" Context

Last weekend, I was at **Majestic** waiting for a bus to Tumkur. I picked up a box of fresh **Mysuru Pak**. As I watched the guy pack them, it hit me—this is the most perfect example of a **Stack** I've ever seen. He keeps placing pieces on top of each other, and when I opened it back at the hostel, my roommate grabbed the one right on top. He didn't dig to the bottom. That’s **LIFO** (Last In, First Out), baby!

---

## 1. So, what's a Stack actually? (ನಮ್ಮ ಭಾಷೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಸಾಫ್ಟ್ ವೇರ್!)

Forget the boring definitions. A stack is basically a "pile." You put things on top (**Push**), and you take things off the top (**Pop**). If you try to take something from the middle, you'll probably break the whole thing—just like a stack of plates at a wedding lunch in Palace Grounds.

**ಸಿಂಪಲ್ ಆಗಿ ಹೇಳ್ಬೇಕಂದ್ರೆ:** ಸ್ಟ್ಯಾಕ್ ಅಂದ್ರೆ ಒಂದು ಸಾಲು ಅಥವಾ ಅಟ್ಟಿ. ಕೊನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇಟ್ಟಿದ್ದನ್ನೇ ಮೊದಲು ತೆಗಿತೀವಿ. ಇದನ್ನೇ ಇಂಜಿನಿಯರಿಂಗ್ ಭಾಷೆಯಲ್ಲಿ **LIFO** ಅಂತಾರೆ. 

---

## 2. Real Talk: Where do we use this?

As engineers, we don't just build sweet boxes. We build systems.
- **Undo Button**: Ever wonder how Ctrl+Z works? Every time you type, your computer "pushes" that action onto a stack. When you undo, it "pops" the last action. Simple, right?
- **The Back Button**: Your browser history is just a giant stack. The last page you visited is the first one you go back to.
- **Recursion**: If you've ever felt your brain heating up during recursion, blame the **Call Stack**. It's the computer's way of remembering "where was I?" before it got into a nested function.

**ಇಂಜಿನಿಯರಿಂಗ್‌ನಲ್ಲಿ ಇದರ ಕಿಮ್ಮತ್ತು:**
- ನಮ್ ಸ್ಮಾರ್ಟ್ ಫೋನ್ ನಲ್ಲಿ 'Back' ಬಟನ್ ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡೋದು ಇದೇ ತತ್ವದ ಮೇಲೆ.
- ಕಂಪ್ಯೂಟರ್ ನಲ್ಲಿ ಏನಾದ್ರೂ ತಪ್ಪಾದ್ರೆ 'Undo' ಮಾಡೋದಕ್ಕೂ ಸ್ಟ್ಯಾಕ್ ಬೇಕೇ ಬೇಕು.
